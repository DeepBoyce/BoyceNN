New York High School graduates have to re-do their final year

By Evan Durgotchia, CNN

Updated 2:16 PM ET, Thur August 26, 2021

(CNN) - High School seniors throughout the five boroughs will have to abruptly put a hold on their future plans as a citywide mandate goes into effect September 27th. Due to the underwhelming academic performance of the 2020-2021 school year ELABORATE ON WHAT BAD GRADES THEY GOT

New York Governor Kathy Hochul and New York City Mayor Bill DeBlasio sign the unprecedented mandate, adding another chapter to NYC's tumultuous handling of the COVID-19 pandemic. REASONING FORM THE GOVERNOR

This change is sure to create a logjam for not only the future of many young students and their families, but for hundreds of universities across the country. The city and state are set to recompense universities for the delay, a move that has upset many. "Colleges have millions in their pockets and already have our money deposited for when our kids actually enter" said Margaret Carter, parent of a 2021 graduate. "They really need to plan things a lot better.. this city is a mess right now" continued Carter.

The university relief has passed through New York State legislation and the fast sweeping mandate has taken many by surprise, "I come back from my vacation and get blindsided by this news! It was bad enough dealing with my oldest being stuck on campus for quarantin during the holidays last year" says Jerry DuMas. "My business is struggling as is, this only adds a ton more stress to my life" continued DuMas.

Former New York City Mayoral candidate Andrew Yang has thrown his hat in support of the thousands of New Yorkers who will be affected by this sudden change. "I stand in support of all of the students who will not only amass tons of debt from higher education costs, but also continue to have their futures in flux due to the incompetence and mishandling of the government" said Yang. Yang goes on saying "People may see me as an opportunist, but as long as the Yang gang has my back I'll continue to do whats right for those that can't do for themselves. I'll be holding a presser at my favorite local bodega to elaborate on all of this".
